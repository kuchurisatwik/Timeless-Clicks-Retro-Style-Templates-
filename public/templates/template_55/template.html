SUPERMAN
INTERVENES IN FOREIGN WAR:
SAVIOR OR ROGUE FORCE?
By Lois Lane, Senior Reporter
In a rare and emotionally charged sit-down, Superman spoke for the first time about his controversial intervention in the recent conflict that erupted in Eastern Vlatava last month — an act that has ignited debate on Capitol Hill, among international leaders, and right here at home.
The Man of Steel's unauthorized entry into the sovereign nation's airspace was widely reported after satellite footage showed him disarming a fleet of armed drones seconds before they would strike a civilian zone. No official military or governmental body claimed coordination with the hero, and some lawmakers are now calling his actions a violation of international law.
When asked if he regrets intervening in what many have called a "delicate geopolitical matter," Superman paused before replying:
"PEOPLE WHERE GOING TO DIE"
-SUPERMAN
The intervention reportedly saved hundreds — possibly thousands — of civilians, including a refugee convoy caught between opposing forces. But the fallout has been severe. The United Nations has issued a formal request for Superman to appear before a multinational oversight committee. Meanwhile, public opinion remains sharply divided.
"He's not a soldier. He doesn't answer to any chain of command," said Senator Rodriguez, Chair of the Defense Subcommittee. "If we praise this now, what happens next time?"
Others, like Coast City's Mayor Langstrom, see things differently. "The man saved lives. I wish we had more of that kind of courage in our own governments."
This moment may define not just Superman's place in the world — but how the world defines heroism going forward.
